Media Recomendations

Please enjoy these Japanese media recomendations ranked from easiest to hardest difficulty.

Please Note: I did not create the descriptions or rankings of the Media recomendations listed below. As this was a project of Kuri(栗) and many other members from "TheMoeWay" discord server (A community & guide where students can learn Japanese the right way!)

Name Ranking Difficulty Description Picture
わかば＊ガール Very Easy Wakaba speaks in keigo but this is the only difficulty, especially considering the episodes are just shorts. Wakaba Kohashi, a sheltered rich girl, dreams of becoming a fashionable and trendy gyaru because she admires their outgoing and carefree nature. With this goal in mind, Wakaba begins the school year hoping to make her high school debut as a gyaru. On the first day of class, she meets the pure Moeko Tokita, the serious Nao Mashiba, and the eccentric Mao Kurokawa, and the four of them quickly become friends as they learn about and imitate each other's lifestyles.
ご注文はうさぎですか？ Very Easy Nothing much to say here, very simple slice of life. Even if you don't understand a particular dialogue you can still get healed by the cuteness. Sugar sweet slice of life, probably one of the best example ever of cute girls doing cute things. Girl moves in to a new town, decides to work as a waitress in a cute café with other cute girls.
しろくまカフェ Very Easy It's very easy because all the dialogues are slow, use only basic grammar, and the topics are almost all about slice-of-life content. It revolves around two stories each episode, so the predictability of the narrative is very short with the beginning, middle, and end of a narrative in less than 15 minutes each. Situated near the local zoo and owned by the charismatic polar bear Shirokuma, Shirokuma Cafe is a popular spot for animals and humans alike, allowing them to sit back and relax after a hard day of work. Whether it's a cold beverage or the latest item on his menu, Shirokuma finds joy in being able to serve his customers, often striking up conversations about various subjects.
けいおん! Easy Easy cute girls doing cute things anime, some music vocabulary but not much. Good vibes, even if you don't get anything you'll still feel all warm and fuzzy inside. One of the original CGDCT anime, protagonist decides to join the light music club and forms an awesome band with friends. They make music, drink tea, eat sweets and have fun.
こみっくがーるず Easy Cute girls doing cute things (CGDCT) with easy to understand language. Kaoruko "Chaos" Moeta is a young manga artist who is down on her luck. She wants to draw manga about high school girls, but her storyboards are bland, her art uninspired, and her premises weak. Her concerned, exasperated editor comes up with an idea: push Chaos to be more social. So, by her recommendation, Chaos moves into a dormitory for female manga artists.
ゆるキャン△ Easy Dialogue is easy, the important parts are the atmosphere, mood and visuals. Extremely comfy CGDCT story about camping. Will make you feel all warm and fuzzy inside.
のんのんびより Easy Easy dialogue, the pace is great and the characters don't speak too fast either. The ultimate comfy anime, Tokyo girl comes to the countryside and cute girls end up doing cute things.
聲の形 Easy Made it easier from the fact that they sometimes use their hands to communicate. It's a film so it's not too long either which helps. As a wild youth, elementary school student Shouya Ishida sought to beat boredom in the cruelest ways. When the deaf Shouko Nishimiya transfers into his class, Shouya and the rest of his class thoughtlessly bully her for fun. However, when her mother notifies the school, he is singled out and blamed for everything done to her, with Shouko transferring out of the school. Sincerely regretting his past actions, he sets out on a journey of redemption in his third year: to meet Shouko once more and make amends.
ゆるゆり Easy Very easy SoL vocabulary, recommended for a beginner. Classic cute girls doing cute things with a slight yuri twist.
からかい上手の高木さん Easy It uses simple expressions, easy grammar and both protagonists speak slowly so you can grasp the meaning of words very easily. With a smirk, the mischievous middle school boy Nishikata glances over at Takagi, the girl seated next to him in class. "If you blush, you lose." That has been the unwritten rule set between the two ever since they encountered one another in middle school. Day after day, loss after loss, Nishikata strives to see Takagi red with embarrassment, but his futile attempts are only met by further ridicule.
女神のカフェテラス Easy It's easy because all of the dialogues are simple, most of the words are very common, and the grammar is very basic. It's not very easy because sometimes the dialogue between the girls and the guy can overlap, becoming a fast-paced dialogue. I would definitely recommend it for beginners, but the speed of the dialogue is certainly higher than, say, Takagi San. After his grandmother Sachiko passes away, Hayato Kasukabe returns from Tokyo and inherits her cafe—Familia Café Terrace. He aims to demolish the cafe and convert it into a parking lot, only to discover that his grandmother had taken in five girls prior to her death. They convince Hayato to change his mind, albeit reluctantly. Through this encounter, Hayato eventually remembers his past with his grandmother as well as his love for the cafe.
たまこまーけっと Easy Simple slice of life/CGDCT vocabulary, nothing too hard going on here. Inside the Usagiyama Shopping District lies an eccentric but close-knit community of business owners. Tamako Kitashirakawa, a clumsy though adorable teenage girl, belongs to a family of mochi bakers who own a quaint shop called Tama-ya. Meanwhile, Tamako's friend, Mochizou Ooji, continues to hide his true feelings for her. Their fathers are fierce mochi rivals, but will it be enough to drive a wedge between Tamako and Mochizou?
あの日見た花の名前を僕達はまだ知らない。 Easy Simple slice of life, no specific terminology, half of the show is kids speaking so it's easier to understand, hardest part is not crying at the end. Jinta Yadomi and his group of childhood friends have become estranged after a tragic accident split them apart. Now in their high school years, a sudden surprise forces each of them to confront their guilt over what happened that day and come to terms with the ghosts of their past.
宇宙よりも遠い場所 Easy Outside of some characters speaking a bit fast from time to time, this is mostly easy. Filled with an overwhelming sense of wonder for the world around her, Mari Tamaki has always dreamt of what lies beyond the reaches of the universe. Still, her fear continues to prevent her from taking that ambitious step forward—that is, until she has a chance encounter with a girl who has grand dreams of her own. Spurred by her mother's disappearance, Shirase Kobuchizawa has been working hard to fund her trip to Antarctica in search of her mother.
ぼくたちは勉強ができない Easy Very simple harem SoL stuff, nothing hard here. Nariyuki Yuiga, an impoverished third-year high school student, works tirelessly to receive the VIP nomination, a scholarship that would cover all of his college tuition fees. In recognition of his hard work, the headmaster awards him the renowned scholarship.However, this scholarship is given under one condition: he must tutor the school's geniuses in their weakest subjects! >
僕は友達が少ない Easy Once again a standard slice of life romcom harem anime. Nothing too spectacular to note here, all the characters speak pretty clearly and the subject matter isn't hard either. When Kodaka Hasegawa finds out that he will be transferring to a new school, he is determined to make a positive impression, and maybe even some friends. However, Kodaka discovers he is out of luck when he immediately gets labeled as a violent delinquent due to his blond hair and intimidating expression. However, his life begins to change when he finds fellow loner Yozora Mikazuki talking to her imaginary friend in an empty classroom.
干物妹！うまるちゃん Easy Standard slice of life fare, Umaru can speak a bit fast in certain parts and her friend has an accent from time to time. Other than this, nothing much to worry about. People are not always who they appear to be, as is the case with Umaru Doma, the perfect high school girl—that is, until she gets home! Once the front door closes, the real fun begins. When she dons her hamster hoodie, she transforms from a refined, over-achieving student into a lazy, junk food-eating otaku, leaving all the housework to her responsible older brother Taihei.
阿波連さんははかれない Easy There is nothing too difficult to see here, it's mostly slice of life vocabulary with some occasional tangents by the main character when he's in delusion-mode looking at Aharen-san. Beginning his first year of high school, all Matsuboshi Raidou wants is to make friends—starting with the cute, tiny, and soft-spoken Reina Aharen, who sits right next to him in class. Unbeknownst to Raidou, Reina shares the same sentiment, but she has a problem. Awkward and timid, Reina is incapable of determining how chummy she has to be when approaching a person.
トニカクカワイイ Easy Very rarely some business terms are used but it's mostly simple romcom vocabulary and sentence structure. On his way home one snowy evening, Yuzaki Nasa's eyes fall upon a peerless beauty across the street. Bewitched, Nasa tries to approach her—only to get blindsided by an oncoming truck. Thankfully, his life is spared due to the girl's swift action. Refusing to let this chance meeting end, he forces his crippled body to chase after her and asks her out. Surprised by his foolhardiness and pure resolve, the girl accepts his confession under a single condition: they can only be together if he marries her!
Kanon Easy Pretty simple, it's a standard dating sim adaptation. The plot becomes important in the latter part of the anime so it's slightly more difficult than general slice of life anime, but it's still easy. As a young child, Aizawa Yuuichi had often visited his cousin in the city; however, something drastic happened to keep him away for seven long years. Now, Yuuichi returns, his memories of those days are simply gone. Settling into the wintry town, Yuuichi comes across several young girls, all of whom are connected to his past. As he befriends them and continues to interact with them, the long forgotten memories from his childhood begin to resurface...
お兄ちゃんだけど愛さえあれば関係ないよねっ Easy Simple harem romcom, the dialogue isn't too hard and the vocabulary is simple. After their parents' deaths, brother and sister Akito and Akiko Himenokouji were forced to live with separate families for six years. But now they have finally reunited and begin to live together. It quickly becomes apparent that Akiko harbors romantic feelings for her brother; however three more girls move into their apartment and the girls start to fight over who should take care of Akito.
WORKING!! Easy Slice of life full of comedy so the vocabulary and grammar aren't too hard. There's very little plot until later on which means you can just enjoy the comedy without caring too much at first. Due to his love for small, cute things, Souta Takanashi cannot turn childlike Popura Taneshima down when she recruits him to work for Wagnaria, a family restaurant located in Hokkaido. Takanashi quickly learns he must stay on his toes once he meets his colleagues, including the katana-wielding Yachiyo Todoroki, the intimidating Jun Satou, the dangerously well-informed Hiroomi Souma, the adamantly lazy manager Kyouko Shirafuji, and the waitress Mahiru Inami who has a "painful" fear of men.
まじもじるるも Easy Pretty easy slice of life anime with a slight fantasy twist. The dialogues are easy and the plot isn't too important outside of the last few episodes. After an unfortunate accident, high school student Kouta Shibaki is branded as the school pervert. With girls avoiding Kouta like the plague, truly the young man's worst nightmare has come to fruition! One day in the school library, he stumbles upon a peculiar book said to possess the power to summon witches. Partly out of desperation, partly out of boredom, Kouta decides to play along with the joke of a book, until an apprentice witch going by the name of Rurumo Maji Mojiruka appears before him.
バクマン。 Easy Some manga terminology, the rest is pretty easy since it is a slice of life mostly. As a child, Moritaka Mashiro dreamt of becoming a mangaka. But when tragedy strikes, he gives up on his dream and spends his middle school days studying instead. One day, his classmate Akito Takagi notices the detailed drawings in Moritaka's notebook. Seeing the vast potential of his artistic talent, Akito approaches Moritaka, proposing that they become mangaka together. Thus the pair begins creating manga hoping to become the greatest mangaka in Japan.
Buddy Daddies Easy Mostly slice of life vocab and dialogue I don't remember anything that was too difficult. On Christmas Eve, four-year-old Miri Unasaka arrives in Tokyo completely alone, in search of her father. The bright lights and merry atmosphere guide Miri to a big hotel and a man with a delicious-looking cake. However, the child has just unknowingly walked into the center of an elaborate, foolproof plan for assassinating a dangerous mafia boss.
可愛いだけじゃない式守さん Easy Slice of life vocabulary, nothing too hard to see here. Yuu Izumi leads a high school life filled with one mishap after another. No matter how improbable the situation, unfortunate events strike him at every turn. In possession of such terrible luck, Izumi enters his second year with a single wish in mind—to spend more time with his affectionate girlfriend, Micchon Shikimori. Since they began dating a year ago, Izumi has witnessed a surprising side to his otherwise adorable girlfriend: when the need arises, she turns incredibly cool!
月がきれい Easy Simple romance story with no real twists so very easy to follow. Characters use simple daily life language. Kotarou Azumi meets Akane Mizuno, a girl from his class, while eating out with his family and from then on the two of them slowly grow their relationship.
コタローは一人暮らし Easy Slice of life without too much complicated vocab, though the main character kotarou speaks weird might be a bit hard to get used to but it's not too bad. One day, manga author Shin Karino is greeted by four-year-old Kotarou Satou, his new next-door neighbor, who gifts him a box of tissues. In Karino's eyes, Kotarou is an odd kid: he speaks in an extremely formal manner, and he lives alone in his apartment—no parents or relatives in sight. But Kotarou neither seems to mind, nor wants to rely on people.
ゲーマーズ！ Easy A lot of gamer slang is used, but the rest is fine. Keita Amano is a typical high school gamer living out an average student's life. One day, however, he has an unexpected meeting with the cutest girl in school that makes him want to disappear without a trace! She discovers that Amano is a gamer, and this newfound knowledge incites a passionate desire within her to recruit him into the game club. Upon visiting the club, Amano is forcefully made aware of a side to gaming wildly different than the one he loves so dearly.
ヒナまつり Easy-Medium Extremely funny with simple dialogues. The only difficulty is the slight yakuza slang from time to time. Other than that, decent especially since Hina speaks slowly in a calm voice. While reveling in the successful clinching of a prized vase for his collection, Yoshifumi Nitta, a yakuza member, is rudely interrupted when a large, peculiar capsule suddenly materializes and falls on his head. He opens the capsule to reveal a young, blue-haired girl, who doesn't divulge anything about herself but her name—Hina—and the fact that she possesses immense powers. ¨Faced with no other choice, Nitta finds himself becoming her caregiver.
四月は君の嘘 Easy-Medium A lot of music words but other than that the conversations are pretty easy. Kousei Arima is a child prodigy known as the "Human Metronome" for playing the piano with precision and perfection. Guided by a strict mother and rigorous training, Kousei dominates every competition he enters, earning the admiration of his musical peers and praise from audiences. When his mother suddenly passes away, the subsequent trauma makes him unable to hear the sound of a piano, and he never takes the stage thereafter.
はじめの一歩 THE FIGHTING! Easy-Medium Main difficulty comes from the boxing terms but they are explained accordingly, so it's still decently easy. In his father's absence, teenager Ippo Makunouchi works hard to help his mother run her fishing boat rental business. Mamoru Takamura, an up-and-coming boxer, rescues Ippo from a violent after-school incident and takes him back to the Kamogawa Boxing Gym for recovery. Takamura is stunned by Ippo's powerful punches. As Ippo takes the first steps in his official boxing career, he faces off against a series of challenging opponents, each more powerful than the last.
日常 Easy-Medium Overall not too hard, although some references can be hard to catch. Even if you don't understand anything you can usually get what's going on from the visuals. You can watch this without understanding anything and still have a good time. Extremely well (some would say over-) animated comedy anime about the daily lives of high school girls. Lots of surreal comedy.
BNA ビー・エヌ・エー Easy-Medium It has some sci-fi/scientific explainations but other than that it is pretty pretty simple. Throughout history, humans have been at odds with Beastmen. After witnessing an explosion in the square of a Beastman city, Michiru Kagemori is confronted by Shirou Ogami, a seemingly indestructible wolf and sworn protector of all Beastmen. As they pursue the criminals behind the bombing, the two discover that Michiru is anything but an ordinary Beastman, and look to investigate her mysterious past and uncanny abilities.
とらドラ！ Easy-Medium Mostly SoL/romance vocabulary. Nothing too hard to follow, though there is an underlying plot. Ryuuji Takasu is a gentle high school student with a love for housework; but in contrast to his kind nature, he has an intimidating face. On the other hand is Taiga Aisaka, a small, doll-like student, who is anything but a cute and fragile girl. Ryuuji discovers that Taiga has a crush on the popular vice president, Yuusaku Kitamura, who happens to be his best friend. But Ryuuji then reveals that he has a crush on Minori Kushieda, Taiga's best friend!
だがしかし Easy-Medium A few dagashi words but nothing too hard. Out in the countryside stands a sweet shop run by the Shikada family for nine generations: Shikada Dagashi, a small business selling traditional Japanese candy. However, despite his father's pleas, Kokonotsu Shikada, an aspiring manga artist, adamantly refuses to inherit the family business. However, this may start to change with the arrival of the eccentric Hotaru Shidare.
中二病でも恋がしたい! Easy-Medium The only hard parts are when the characters go into chuunibyou mode. The rest of the dialogue is standard slice of life romcom stuff, so nothing too hard. If you want to understand everything, it's closer to medium. Everybody has had that stage in their life where they have thought themselves to be special, different from the masses of ordinary humans. They might go as far as seeing themselves capable of wielding mystical powers, or maybe even believe themselves to have descended from a fantasy realm. This "disease" is known as "chuunibyou" and is often the source of some of the most embarrassing moments of a person's life. For Yuuta Togashi, the scars that his chuunibyou has left behind are still fresh.
魔法少女まどか★マギカ Easy-Medium Deconstruction of the magical girl genre which means you get some fantasy vocabulary but nothing much, mostly mahou shoujo/witch stuff. It's not too hard but the plot is important so if you miss stuff you'll be confused. Madoka Kaname and Sayaka Miki are regular middle school girls with regular lives, but all that changes when they encounter Kyuubey, a cat-like magical familiar, and Homura Akemi, the new transfer student. Kyuubey offers them to grant any one of their wishes and in exchange, they will each become a magical girl, gaining enough power to fulfill their dreams. However, Homura Akemi, a magical girl herself, urges them not to accept the offer, stating that everything is not what it seems.
坂道のアポロン Easy-Medium The vocabulary and sentence structures aren't very hard, although there is obviously some jazz terminology thrown in there. Introverted classical pianist and top student Kaoru Nishimi has just arrived in Kyushu for his first year of high school. Having constantly moved from place to place since his childhood, he abandons all hope of fitting in, preparing himself for another lonely, meaningless year. That is, until he encounters the notorious delinquent Sentarou Kawabuchi whose love of jazz inspires him to start playing with him.
斉木楠雄のΨ難 Easy-Medium The characters can talk a bit fast but other than this should be fine. To the average person, psychic abilities might seem a blessing; for Kusuo Saiki, however, this could not be further from the truth. Gifted with a wide assortment of supernatural abilities ranging from telepathy to x-ray vision, he finds this so-called blessing to be nothing but a curse. As all the inconveniences his powers cause constantly pile up, all Kusuo aims for is an ordinary, hassle-free life—a life where ignorance is bliss.
ぼっち・ざ・ろっく！ Easy-Medium Mostly slice of life conversations, however there is some music terminology that are used and many jokes, some of which may be hard to understand. One day, skilled guitarist Hitori Gotou meets drummer Nijika and is invited to join "Kessoku Band". Together with the other two band members they create various songs and host many performances.
無職転生 Easy-Medium Nothing too difficult, standard fantasy setting with standard fantasy vocabulary. When a 34-year-old underachiever gets run over by a bus, his story doesn't end there. Reincarnated as an infant, Rudy will use newfound courage, friends, and magical abilities to embark on an epic adventure!
Bartender Easy-Medium The anime is episodical so even if you don't understand one episode, the next one is mostly not affected. Some cocktail terminology you need to pick up but the leisurely pace and mood make it easier. Hidden in the backstreets of the Ginza district is Eden Hall, a lone bar operated by Ryuu Sasakura, the prodigy bartender who is said to mix the most incredible cocktails anyone has ever tasted. However, not just anyone can find Eden Hall; rather, it is Eden Hall that must find you. Customers of varying backgrounds, each plagued with their own troubles, wander into this bar. Nevertheless, Ryuu always knows the ideal cocktail to console and guide each distraught soul.
スラムダンク Easy-Medium The main difficulty comes from the various basketball terms, but they are usually explained when they're introduced which makes it easier. The main character initially dislikes basketball but gets roped into it by his crush and soon learns to enjoy it.
モンスター Medium The difficulty varies from episode to episode, with some of them having a lot more medical jargon and philosophical terms. Dr. Kenzou Tenma, an elite neurosurgeon recently engaged to his hospital director's daughter, is well on his way to ascending the hospital hierarchy. While preparing to perform surgery on someone, he gets a call from the hospital director telling him to switch patients and instead perform life-saving brain surgery on a famous performer. Because of the switch, a poor immigrant worker is dead, causing Dr. Tenma to have a crisis of conscience.
プロメア Medium Sci-fi action anime film, has some challenging bits but overall is fairly normal. The Burnish are a group of humans with the ability to create bend fire to their will. Burnish first appeared 30 years ago, when an alternate dimension meshed with the one earth resides in. Sentient beings from the alternate dimension called Promare became trapped on Earth. The Promare has a desire to burn as that is how they thrive. They latch onto humans for hosts, and obey their host's will, creating those known as Burnish.
てーきゅう Medium Slice of life shitpost anime, episodes are super short so easy to digest. The story follows the everyday lives of four girls of the Kameido High School's tennis club. The series' focus is not really on tennis, but on the wacky and eccentric characters, with strange object associations and even stranger misconceptions, on their many random adventures.
ヴィンランド・サガ Medium Harder than basic romcoms and the likes, but not too difficult either. Has some fantasy/historical vocabulary. Young Thorfinn grew up listening to the stories of old sailors that had traveled the ocean and reached the place of legend, Vinland. It's said to be warm and fertile, a place where there would be no need for fighting—not at all like the frozen village in Iceland where he was born, and certainly not like his current life as a mercenary. War is his home now. The war between England and the Danes grows worse with each passing year. Death has become commonplace.
Serial Experiments Lain Medium While the anime itself is hard to understand with a lot of symbolism, the actual speech isn't too hard for a good part of the anime. Some of the latter scenes can be a bit harder to understand, but the language used isn't too difficult. Lain receives a strange e-mail from a classmate of her who commited suicide last week. When she decides to open it, it leads her straight into the Wired, a virtual world of communication networks similar to what we know as the internet. With the boundaries between reality and cyberspace rapidly blurring, Lain is plunged into more surreal and bizarre events where identity, consciousness, and perception are concepts that take on new meanings.
ハンター×ハンター Medium The arcs themselves can vary a lot in difficulty, from easy-medium to medium-hard so medium seems decent as a difficulty marker. There's shonen lingo as well as general fantasy vocabulary. Hunters devote themselves to accomplishing hazardous tasks, all from traversing the world's uncharted territories to locating rare items and monsters. Before becoming a Hunter, one must pass the Hunter Examination—a high-risk selection process in which most applicants end up handicapped or worse, deceased. Believing that he will meet his father by becoming a Hunter, 12-year old Gon Freecs takes the first step to walk the same path.
デス・パレード Medium The main character uses keigo and the subject matter is more complex than basic slice of life or romcom anime, but it is still not extremely difficult. While the plot is important, the various games are interesting on their own. After death, either Heaven or Hell awaits most humans. But for a select few, death brings them to Quindecim—a bar where only pairs of people who die at the same time can enter. Attending the bar is an enigmatic figure known as Decim, who also acts as the arbiter. He passes judgment on those who wind up at Quindecim by challenging them to a life-threatening game. These games determine if the patron's soul will reincarnate into a new life, or be sent into the void, never to be seen again.
ピンポン THE ANIMATION Medium While the vocabulary isn't too complicated, some characters speak in Chinese with Japanese subtitles and the dialogues can get a bit philosophical at times. Obviously there's some ping pong terminology, although ping pong isn't actually the main theme of this anime. Despite being polar opposites, Smile and Peco have been best friends since childhood. Although the overly confident Peco strives to be the best ping-pong player in the world, he often skips practice. Meanwhile, Smile cannot help but hold back his full strength when playing against others. But when these young athletes let their unbridled ambition go unchecked, the hardships they face paint a somber reality as they pursue glory.
地獄学園 Medium One character speaks like a samurai, there is some wordplay and the characters can talk a bit fast at times. Overall not too hard, but not quite very easy either. Still, the comedy is so good that even if you don't fully understand what's going on you might like it nonetheless. Located on the outskirts of Tokyo, Hachimitsu Private Academy is a prestigious all-girls boarding school. Due to the revision of the school's most iconic policy, as boys are now able to enroll as well. Five boys are accepted but try to peep and get thrown into the school's prison with the sentence of an entire month as punishment. Thus begins the tale of the boys' harsh lives in Prison School, a righteous struggle that will ultimately test the bonds of friendship and perverted brotherhood.
ブルーロック Medium Some characters have their own speaking style where add things to the ends of their sentences or use slightly rarer words so it can be a touch harder to pick up on what they say. In 2018, the Japanese national team finished 16th in the FIFA World Cup. As a result, the Japan Football Association hires the football enigma Ego Jinpachi. His master plan to lead Japan to stardom is Blue Lock, a training regimen designed to create the world's greatest egotist striker. Yoichi Isagi, an unknown high school football player who is conflicted about his playing style, decides to join the program in order to become the best egotistical striker in the world.
闘牌伝説アカギ闇に舞い降りた天才 Medium Knowing Mahjong is an absolute requirement for this one or you will be completely lost. Other than this, medium difficulty. While mahjong is a game that is often played with family and friends, it is also a game that is played in the darkest corners of society. Nangou is a compulsive gambler who has accumulated debt over three million yen. In a last ditch attempt to clear his record, he decides to wager his life on a game of mahjong with the mafia. Unfortunately, as the game progresses, Nangou only moves further from the prize and closer to death.
逆境無頼カイジ Ultimate Survivor Medium-Hard Just like above (it's the same author), this centers on mahjong. It is slightly more difficult though, because you need to pretty accurately understand the rules of each gamble in order to fully enjoy it and there is fairly uncommon vocabulary used by the narrator in Kaiji's particular style of writing. After one of his coworkers fails to repay a debt, apathetic bum Kaiji Itou is pressured by loan sharks to settle the large sum of cash. Since Kaiji has little money and no future prospects of his own, he is unable to return what his colleague borrowed. Faced with massive financial burdens, Kaiji is given an offer to board the illustrious Espoir—it is said that "one night aboard the ship, and all of your debts will be paid."
約束のネバーランド Medium-Hard The grammar is very complicated sometimes, and the vocabulary is not that easy. However, the narrative is clear as water, and the dialogues are very well written. It seems like you're reading a novel only made with dialogues. With Violet, even if the vocabulary is sometimes abstract (because of the story), you can almost always keep understanding because of the clearly written dialogue. Surrounded by a forest and a gated entrance, the Grace Field House is inhabited by orphans happily living together as one big family, looked after by their "Mama," Isabella. Although they are required to take tests daily, the children are free to spend their time as they see fit, usually playing outside, as long as they do not venture too far from the orphanage. However, all good times must come to an end, as every few months, a child is adopted and sent to live with their new family, never to be heard from again.
新世紀エヴァンゲリオン Medium-Hard "The anime varies in difficult, in some episodes it is rather easy, towards the end it becomes pretty difficult with intricate dialogue discussing somewhat deep ideas. The mecha and technological terms can be a bit hard at first as well. The rebuilds (movies for Evangelion) are roughly of the same difficulty." Evangelion is set fifteen years after a worldwide cataclysm named Second Impact, particularly in the futuristic fortified city of Tokyo-3. The protagonist is Shinji Ikari, a teenage boy who is recruited by his father Gendo to the shadowy organization Nerv to pilot a giant bio-machine mecha named Evangelion into combat against beings known as Angels.
魔法科高校の劣等生 Medium-Hard Miyuki speaks in very polite speech, lots of technical words, the magic system is very complete but also technological in nature and thus sometimes a bit hard to follow. The story takes place in an alternate history where magic exists and is polished through technology, and follows Tatsuya and Miyuki Shiba, siblings who enroll into First High magic high school.
